Coylean Globe

The Coylean map wound onto a sphere with no longitude repetition — each column spans 2π/D radians, so spinning east raises the winding number τ without bound and every turn exposes new map. Two builds of the same idea:

Coylean Globedescent · current

The unbounded, instant build. Cells come from substitution address descent off a tiny seed (superglyphs/cell-descent.mjs) — no boundary propagation, no lazy tile build, no extent cap. Deep zoom and hundreds of thousands of non-repeating turns. Orientation (Lat/Long/Sen), branch-cut age tint, two-tier skeleton/texture LOD.

Coylean Globescaffold · old

The original build, kept for comparison. Cells come from the lazy big-map seam scaffold — a centred integrated universe propagated in K×K blocks on demand, with a bounded Extent and an O((2W)²) boundary seed up front. Same winding model and look; slower to fill in and capped in size.
