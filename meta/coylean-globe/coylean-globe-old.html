Unbounded Coylean Globe

The Coylean map wound onto a globe with no longitude repetition. Each column spans 2π/D radians (the same cell scale as the flat map), but successive turns expose new map — spin east and the winding number τ climbs without bound. The branch point (the maximum-priority axis — prime meridian and equator) is parked at the back; the spiral's cut sits there and may peek over the poles.

Latitude is Mercator (square cells), so the fine N–S structure only resolves as you magnify. The map is built lazily from the big-map seam scaffold: tiles propagate on demand where the viewport visits, gated so sub-pixel cells are never built.
