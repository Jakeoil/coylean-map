Reaction Box — the papers

The two write-ups behind the reaction box: The Reaction Box (PDF) — the locally symmetric, information-preserving cellular generator and which other algorithms share that property; and its Appendix (PDF) — the supporting derivations, including the classical-controlled CNOT / quantum-gate framing.

4D Tesseract Viewer

A three.js viewer for the tesseract. Rotate it in the xw, yw, and zw planes and watch its perspective shadow; the rotate sliders also scrub the angles directly when paused. Switch to a 2×2×2×2 grid of 16 face-sharing tesseracts; pop in the x/y/z/w axis overlays with clickable tip spheres that toggle the 8 tesseracts on each side; reveal only the shared 2-faces, color them by which of the 32 shared 3-cells they belong to, or restrict to one or more slice walls (x=0, y=0, z=0, w=0). The "cell markers" toggle places a tiny axis-colored cube at each 3-cell's centroid in its own 3-flat, illustrating the unique 4-coloring where parallel cells share a color.

4D Hyperdiagonal Lattice Viewer

A 2D projection of the 4D integer lattice with hyperdiagonal edges — every point connected to its 16 (±1,±1,±1,±1) neighbors. Points are colored by parity; choose between axis-pair projections, a split xy + zw view, and a simple 4D isometric projection. Cycle the focus point to highlight its star of hyperdiagonals.

Cube Cuts — Recursive Corner Bisection

A precursor to the 4D version: three axis-aligned cuts through a 3D cube, each starting at the midpoint of one of the three edges meeting at a common corner. Every cut only bisects the most recently created sub-cell, so the cube ends up split into four pieces of sizes ½, ¼, ⅛, ⅛. Animate each cut as a diagonal sweep that grows through a triangle, a pentagon (or trapezoid → pentagon for the non-square midplane of cut 2), and finally fills the cut surface. Then explode the four resulting 3-cells outward to see them as separate solids, labeled by tag (1.1.1, 1.1.2, 1.2, 2). Same scheme generalizes to n cuts giving n+1 cells in an n-cube.

Wall-config classifier — 3D reaction box

Step through the 15 wall configurations on the cube's 12 central panels (3 coordinate planes × 4 quadrants each) that partition the 8 octants into rectangular sub-boxes, grouped by the full octahedral symmetry Oh (48 elements). Walled panels are drawn, the resulting rooms are translucently coloured. Classify each orbit into definite / impossible / ambiguous and the rest of the orbit follows.

Reaction Box (3D) — 3-bit priority CNOT

A 3-bit reaction box on the same cube as Cube Cuts, with priority locked at A > B > C (y > x > z). A is red on the y-axis (emitter at top, propagation downward); B is blue on the x-axis (emitter at −x, propagation rightward); C is green on the z-axis (emitter at +z, propagation inward). Highest-priority tape is the widest. Tape visibility = emitter bit; receiver discs track priority3()'s output.

Priority CNOT 3 — testbed

Bare testbed for src/3-bit-priority-cnot-cascade.js. Same cube and discs as Reaction Box, default priority order locked at z > y > x. Click an emitter and watch the receivers update.

Configurable extent, seniority, and an isometric / perspective toggle.
