Ruler grid on a sphere

Longitude lines are dyadic. Latitude circles use Mercator-style spacing on the sphere, so the sectors are locally closer to squares instead of 1×2 rectangles. Line thickness follows the 2-adic ruler pattern: 0, 1, 2, 1, 3, 1, 2, 1, 4…

Capping a priority (the engine's maxLongPri / maxLatPri) clamps that ruler so it repeats every 2cap lines — a seamless cyclic grid with no single distinguished line, the globe's natural longitude wrap. Leave a cap Off for the plain Mercator ruler.

Switch Render to Coylean map to replace the grid with a centred universe (equator + prime meridian at the geometric centre). Longitude cycles over the division (maxLongPri = log2(division)); latitude is the uncapped Mercator axis. Down/right arrows draw as meridian/parallel streamlines at the same density as the grid.
