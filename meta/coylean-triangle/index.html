Coylean Triangle

Take the explorer's propagation raster — red down diamonds and blue right diamonds — and turn it 45° clockwise. Each diamond becomes a square holding one stroke: ╱ for a down arrow, ╲ for a right arrow, and a blank where the diamond was empty. It is a real Coylean square (north = west = 1, so the ∞ frame is in), read over the causal anti-diagonal corner: the west column is all down arrows — a naked slash left edge — and the north row all right arrows — a naked backslash right edge. This first view is typographical — strokes straight from the engine's downMatrix / rightMatrix (the trusted oracle). A pixelated cellular-automaton version, then a universe version, come next.

Down and right, on the diagonal

Each cell (i, j) of the square carries two arrows: a vertical down arrow (downMatrix[j][i]) and a horizontal right arrow (rightMatrix[i][j]). On the anti-diagonal (screen row i + j) the right arrow draws a ╲ on the left of the cell and the down arrow a ╱ on the right — the ╲╱ valley reaction where both fire, a blank where neither does. The priorities that decided those arrows during propagation — downWins = colPriority[i] ≥ rowPriority[j] under vertical seniority, strict > under horizontal, the backslash dual — are already baked into the finished matrices; here we just read them off.

Because it is integrated as a real square with the ∞ frame (northExtent = westExtent = 1), the west column is all down arrows — the naked-slash left edge — and the north row all right arrows — the naked-backslash right edge. In between, the interleaved ruler ∞ · 0 · 1 · 0 · 2 · … is exactly colPriority[i] = pri(i) read along each triangle row — toggle priority labels to tag every ╱ with its column's pri(i) and every ╲ with its row's pri(j) (∞ at the frame).

Oracle, not hand-rolled. The strokes here are a pure re-read of the engine's finished downMatrix / rightMatrix — the Coylean map is bijective, so core is the trusted oracle.
