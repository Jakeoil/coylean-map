Proper Coylean Square — 16×16

This is a proper Coylean Square — not just a finite grid propagated from all-true boundaries. We build a Universe with one cell of context beyond the origin to the north and west and the full 16×16 square to the south and east, then call Propagation.fromUniverseBoundary(universe) to reseed a fresh SE-flowing propagation from the universe's outer N row and W column. The 16×16 SE sector is the proper Coylean Square; the extra row and column at the top and left carry the NW context that produced its seed, so the square is the genuine SE patch of an infinite Coylean map rather than a boundary-clipped approximation.
