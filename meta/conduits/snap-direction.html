The Snap Direction

When a cell snaps, which way does the bar fall? It depends on which way it grew. A v-grow widens the cell and the bar drops vertically — the columns divide, two cells side-by-side (the V → H substitution, 2ⁿ → 2ⁿ⁺¹ columns). An h-grow heightens the cell and the bar lies across — the rows divide, two cells stacked (H → V, rows doubled).

v-grow · split columns

V → H · widen to 3:2 · ∥

h-grow · split rows

H → V · heighten to 3:2 · ═

These are not two rules — they are one move seen through the main diagonal. Reflect the left demo across the backslash and you get the right one exactly: width becomes height, a vertical bar becomes a horizontal one, V becomes H. That is the D₁ transform, and it is the whole reason the map can be unbiased: the growth has no preferred axis, only a preferred diagonal — and the looking-glass swaps even that.

So the ladder V → H → 2V → 2H → … is just the cell alternating which way it last grew. Ask whether v-then-h equals h-then-v and the answer is: yes, up to the backslash. Order only chooses which side of the mirror you read.
