The Morph Unit

The thing that actually breathes and divides is not the whole map — it is a single cell. Hold one in view and the growth is simple: a square stretches along one axis until it reaches 3 : 2, the proportion at which it can no longer pass as a square, and it snaps — a bar drops down the long middle and what was one cell is now two.

1 : 1  →  3 : 2  →  ∥ snap ∥  →  two cells

Each of the two halves is a smaller rectangle (3 : 4), ready to be grown the other way on the next half-order. Run the whole map and every cell is doing this at once, one phase out of step with its neighbours — which is why the map stays a coherent Coylean square instead of a bag of loose tiles.
