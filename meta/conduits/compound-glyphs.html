Compound Glyphs

The real Coylean map on the anchor point (1/1), where the glyph letters are a mnemonic. Each glyph carries a baby-block letter — just the letter part, no border, in black, at the glyph’s own orientation (the blocks were chosen partly for their rough match to the line pattern). The letters are tiny: zoom in to read them. Where neighbors share an edge with no bar, they fuse into one rectangle — a compound glyph, faintly tinted; the rectangle your eye picks out scanning a map.
