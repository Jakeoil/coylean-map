The Unbiased Map

Why should H always play second fiddle? In the usual render the horizontal half-order draws skinny — yet those tiles are fine once you fatten them back into squares. It is only a D₁ backslash transform: H is V seen through the main diagonal. So here we stop squashing. Every cell keeps an honest square and the map grows instead — a square swells toward 3 : 2, then snaps, its long side dividing in two. v-grow splits the columns, h-grow splits the rows, and the two are mirror twins. Agnostic growth. Drink me, eat me — and step through the looking-glass.
